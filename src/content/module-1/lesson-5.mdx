### The "Signal Word" Strategy

Speakers often state a general opinion and then correct themselves. You must wait for the "But".

* **The Trap:** "Am Anfang dachte ich, das Projekt ist toll..." (Listener marks "True: He thinks it's great").
* **The Reality:** "...aber sp√§ter habe ich gemerkt, dass es zu teuer ist." (Answer is "False").

**Watch out for these signal words:**

* *eigentlich* (actually - signals a correction comes next)
* *allerdings* (however)
* *im Gegensatz dazu* (in contrast to that)
* *bisher* (until now - signals a change is coming)

## 3. ‚úçÔ∏è Deep Theory: The Polite "Request" (Indirekte Fragen)

In B2 writing, you cannot say: *"Was kostet der Kurs?"* (That is A1 level and rude).
You must say: *"K√∂nnten Sie mir bitte sagen, wie viel der Kurs kostet?"*

### The Grammar Rules:

1. **Introductory Phrase:** *Ich w√ºsste gern,...* / *K√∂nnten Sie mir mitteilen,...* / *Mich interessiert,...*
2. **The Connector:**
* If there is a question word (W-Wort): Use **wann, wo, wie, warum**.
* If there is NO question word (Yes/No question): Use **ob** (if).


3. **Word Order:** The conjugated verb moves to the **very end** (Kick-to-the-end).
