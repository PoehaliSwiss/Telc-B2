## 1. üìò Deep Theory: Cracking "Sprachbausteine Teil 2"

In this section, you see a text with 10 gaps. You must write **exactly one word** in each gap. Random guessing does not work here; you need to analyze the **Grammar of the Gap**.

### What kind of words are missing?

Statistically, 90% of the missing words fall into four categories:

1. **Prepositions:** (See Lesson 3). Look at the verb or noun before the gap.
* *Example:* "Ich interessiere mich **f√ºr** Sport."


2. **Conjunctions:** (See Lesson 1 & 2). Look at the sentence structure (comma? verb position?).
* *Example:* "Ich komme nicht, **weil** ich krank bin."


3. **Pronouns:** Often Relative Pronouns or Reflexive Pronouns.
* *Example:* "Das ist der Mann, **der** mir geholfen hat." (Relative)
* *Example:* "Ich freue **mich** auf den Urlaub." (Reflexive)


4. **Auxiliary Verbs:** *haben, sein, werden*. Look for a Participle II at the end of the sentence.
* *Example:* "Der Brief **wurde** gestern geschrieben." (Passiv)



### The "Left-Right" Analysis Strategy

Don't read the whole text at once. Focus on the immediate environment of the gap.

* **Look Left:** Is there a verb that needs a preposition? Is there a comma?
* **Look Right:** Is there a noun (check the case)? Is there a verb at the end?

## 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Professional Insight: Strategies & Traps

1. **The "One Word" Rule:**
* You can strictly write only **one** word.
* *Trap:* If you think the answer is "in der", you are wrong (that's two words). The answer is probably a contraction like "im" (in dem) or "zur" (zu der), or the text structure is different than you think.


2. **Relative Pronouns:**
* This is the most common gap type. Check the **Gender** of the noun it refers to, and the **Case** inside the sub-clause.
* *Example:* "Der Mann, **dem** (Dativ) ich helfe."


3. **Reflexive Verbs:**
* Always check if the verb is reflexive.
* *Example:* "Wir haben **uns** gut erholt."

## 4. üó£Ô∏è Speaking Part 1: The Presentation

**Time:** approx. 1.5 minutes.
**Task:** You must speak about a topic (Book, Film, Travel, Event, or Person).
**Structure:** You cannot just ramble. You need a "Roter Faden" (Common thread).

### The 4-Step Structure

1. **Einleitung (Introduction):** State the topic clearly.
2. **Inhalt (Content):** Briefly describe the "What/Where/Who".
3. **Begr√ºndung (Reason):** Why did you choose this? Why was it special? (Emotional connection).
4. **Empfehlung/Schluss (Conclusion):** Would you recommend it?
