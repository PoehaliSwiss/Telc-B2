## 1. üìò Deep Theory: Turning Sentences into Adjectives

Instead of using a Relative Clause (Lesson 12), we turn the verb into an adjective and place it **before** the noun.

### A. Partizip I (Active & Simultaneous)

* **Form:** Verb infinitive + **d** (+ Adjective Ending).
* **Meaning:** Someone/Something is **doing** the action right now.
* **Transformation:**
* *Relativsatz:* Der Hund, **der bellt**. (The dog that barks).
* *Partizip:* Der **bellende** Hund. (The barking dog).



### B. Partizip II (Passive & Completed)

* **Form:** Participle II form (ge-...) (+ Adjective Ending).
* **Meaning:** Something **was done** to the noun.
* **Transformation:**
* *Relativsatz:* Das Auto, **das verkauft wurde**. (The car that was sold).
* *Partizip:* Das **verkaufte** Auto. (The sold car).



### C. The "Extended" Attribute (The Monster)

You can pack extra information between the Article and the Noun. This creates a "Grammar Sandwich".

* **Structure:** [Article] + [Details] + [Participle] + [Noun]
* *Relativsatz:* Das Problem, **das** wir gestern im Meeting **diskutiert haben**.
* *Partizip:* Das [gestern im Meeting] **diskutierte** Problem.
