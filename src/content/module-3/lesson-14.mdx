## 1. üéß Deep Theory: Implicit Meaning & Synonyms

At level B2, the answer is rarely repeated word-for-word from the text. You are testing for **Synonyms** and **Inference** (Logical Conclusion).

### The "Synonym Mapping" Rule

The audio will express the same idea as the correct answer, but using different vocabulary.

* **Audio:** "Die Kosten sind explodiert." (Costs exploded).
* **Correct Option:** "Das Projekt wurde teurer als geplant." (Project became more expensive than planned).
* **Distractor:** "Es gab eine Explosion." (There was an explosion) ‚Äì *This is a trap using the same word!*

### The "Inference" Rule

Sometimes the speaker doesn't say "Yes" or "No". They imply it.

* **Audio:** "Naja, ich h√§tte mir mehr Unterst√ºtzung gew√ºnscht." (Well, I would have wished for more support).
* **Question:** Was the speaker satisfied?
* **Answer:** **No.** (Inference: "Wishing for more" = Not satisfied).

## 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Professional Insight: The "Anchor" Strategy

Since you have two questions per text, you need to know when the answer to Question 1 has passed and the answer to Question 2 is coming.

1. **Read the Questions First:** Identify keywords for Question 2.
2. **Listen for the Transition:** Speakers use "Signpost Words" to move to the next topic.
* *Kommen wir nun zu...* (Let's move on to...)
* *Ein weiterer wichtiger Aspekt ist...* (Another important aspect is...)
* *Zusammenfassend kann man sagen...* (In summary...)
