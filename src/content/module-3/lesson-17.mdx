## 1. üîç The "Dirty Dozen" ‚Äì Most Common B2 Errors

We will focus on the errors that immediately signal to an examiner: "This student is not careful."

### Error Type 1: The "Das" vs. "Dass" Confusion

This is the #1 spelling mistake.

* **Das (Pronoun/Article):** Refers to a noun. Can be replaced by *dieses* or *welches*.
* **Dass (Conjunction):** Connects two sentences. Cannot be replaced.
* *Test:* "Ich hoffe, **dass** das Wetter gut ist." (I hope *that* the weather is good).
* Can I say: "Ich hoffe, *welches*...?" NO. -> **Dass**.


* *Test:* "Das Auto, **das** ich kaufte." (The car *that/which* I bought).
* Can I say: "Das Auto, *welches*...?" YES. -> **Das**.



### Error Type 2: The "Weil" vs. "Denn" Logic

Both mean "because", but the word order is different.

* **Denn:** Position 0. Verb is at Position 2. (*...denn ich **bin** m√ºde.*)
* **Weil:** Subordinate Clause. Verb is at the **End**. (*...weil ich m√ºde **bin**.*)

### Error Type 3: "Seit" vs. "Vor" vs. "F√ºr" (Time)

* **Seit:** Started in the past, **still continues**. (Used with Present Tense!).
* *Wrong:* I am living here since 2 years. -> *Ich wohne hier **seit** 2 Jahren.*


* **Vor:** Happened in the past, **finished**. (Used with Past Tense).
* *Example:* I came here 2 years ago. -> *Ich bin **vor** 2 Jahren gekommen.*


* **F√ºr:** Duration (Planning or Completed).
* *Example:* I am going for 2 weeks. -> *Ich gehe **f√ºr** 2 Wochen.*



### Error Type 4: "Wenn" vs. "Als" (When)

* **Als:** Single event in the **Past**. (*Als ich Kind war...*).
* **Wenn:** Present, Future, or **Repeated** event in the Past (*Immer wenn...*).
* *Mistake:* "**Wenn** ich nach Deutschland kam..." (It happened once).
* *Correction:* "**Als** ich nach Deutschland kam..."

## 3. üïµÔ∏è Simulation: Find the 5 Errors

**Task:** Read the text below. It contains **5 fossilized errors**. Find them mentally, then check the solution.

> **Text:** "Hallo Anna, ich schreibe dir, weil ich habe eine Frage. Das Buch, dass du mir gegeben hast, ist toll. Ich lese es f√ºr drei Stunden jeden Tag. Wenn ich ein Kind war, habe ich nie gelesen. Ich hoffe, das wir uns bald sehen."

**The Correction Analysis:**

1. *Error:* "...weil ich **habe** eine Frage."
* *Fix:* "...weil ich eine Frage **habe**." (Weil kicks verb to end).


2. *Error:* "Das Buch, **dass** du mir..."
* *Fix:* "Das Buch, **das** du mir..." (Relative pronoun referring to Buch).


3. *Error:* "Ich lese es **f√ºr** drei Stunden..."
* *Fix:* "Ich lese es **seit** drei Stunden..." (if still reading) OR just "Ich lese es drei Stunden lang..." (German rarely uses "f√ºr" for duration of action unless purely planning).


4. *Error:* "**Wenn** ich ein Kind war..."
* *Fix:* "**Als** ich ein Kind war..." (Past, single state).


5. *Error:* "Ich hoffe, **das** wir uns..."
* *Fix:* "Ich hoffe, **dass** wir uns..." (Conjunction).

## 5. ‚úçÔ∏è Strategy: Self-Correction in Writing

In the writing exam, always save **5 minutes** at the end for the "Formal Check".
Do not check for content. Check ONLY for:

1. **Verb Position:** Is the verb at the end after *weil/dass/wenn*?
2. **Subject-Verb Agreement:** *Die Leute **sind*** (not ist).
3. **Umlauts:** Did you write *wurde* (past) or *w√ºrde* (conditional)? This changes the meaning completely!

**Visual Check:**

* Look at every comma. Is there a connector (*dass, weil, der/die/das*)? Check the verb at the end of that sentence.
