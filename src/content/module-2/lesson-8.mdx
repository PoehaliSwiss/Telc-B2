## 1. üìò Deep Theory: Action vs. State

In B2, you must distinguish between the **Action** (Someone is doing it) and the **Result** (It is done).

### A. Vorgangspassiv (Process Passive)

* **Focus:** The action itself. Who did it is often unimportant.
* **Formula:** `werden` (conjugated) + ... + `Partizip II`.
* **Agents:**
* **von (+ Dat):** People or Institutions (The "Doer"). -> *Das Auto wird **vom Mechaniker** repariert.*
* **durch (+ Akk):** Means, instruments, or causes. -> *Das Haus wurde **durch ein Feuer** zerst√∂rt.*



### B. Zustandspassiv (State Passive)

* **Focus:** The result *after* the action.
* **Formula:** `sein` (conjugated) + ... + `Partizip II`.
* **Example:**
* *Action:* "Die T√ºr wird geschlossen." (Someone is closing it right now).
* *Result:* "Die T√ºr **ist** geschlossen." (It is closed).



### C. The Tense Trap (Zeitformen)

You must master the Past Tense forms for the exam.

| Tense | Active | Passive (Process) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pr√§sens** | Man operiert ihn. | Er **wird** operiert. |
| **Pr√§teritum** | Man operierte ihn. | Er **wurde** operiert. (Most common in texts!) |
| **Perfekt** | Man hat ihn operiert. | Er **ist** operiert **worden**. (Note: *worden*, not *geworden*!) |
| **Mit Modalverb** | Man muss ihn operieren. | Er **muss** operiert **werden**. |

## 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Professional Insight: Exam Traps

1. **"Worden" vs. "Geworden":**
* *Passiv Perfekt:* "Das Auto ist repariert **worden**." (Correct).
* *Aktiv Perfekt:* "Er ist Arzt **geworden**." (He became a doctor).
* In *Sprachbausteine*, if you see "ist ... Partizip II ... [gap]", check if it's Passive (*worden*) or "Becoming" (*geworden*).


2. **Zustandspassiv in Reading:**
* Often used in descriptions: "Das B√ºro **ist** ge√∂ffnet." (Opened). Don't look for an agent; it's a state.

## 4. ‚úçÔ∏è Writing Strategy: Objective Reporting

In a **Beschwerde** (Complaint), using the Passive makes you sound less aggressive and more professional.

* *Aggressive (Active):* "Sie haben das Zimmer nicht geputzt!" (You didn't clean the room!)
* *Professional (Passive):* "Das Zimmer **wurde nicht geputzt**." (The room was not cleaned).
